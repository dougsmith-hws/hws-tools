IRR (Internal Rate of Return) accounts for when money goes in and comes out — making it the most accurate way to compare investment property returns. It asks: what annual return would make the NPV of all cash flows equal zero?
Cash-on-Cash is simpler: annual net cash flow ÷ total cash invested.
